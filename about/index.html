--- layout: default title: Samara Mendez ---

    I am the economist for Humane League Labs, and I recently defended my dissertation as a PhD candidate at the University of Colorado, Boulder. I am interested in research topics at the intersection of Industrial Organization and Environmental/Agricultural Economics, with an emphasis on food policy. My current research focuses on the effects of taxation and labeling policies in imperfectly competitive industries. I have expertise in structural estimation and applied theoretical modeling. My research agenda includes understanding the distributional and market structure effects of food taxes and investigating economic policies for innovative foods.

    I am originally from Austin, Texas.
